Frequently Asked Questions

  • What kind of people look for coaches?

    People looking to achieve more out of their personal and professional lives are greatly benefited from getting a coach. They may come from different backgrounds, but they all have this common desire to do better. It could be a college student searching for a career that is right for him. Or it could be an employee who wants to give and get more from her work. It could be a homemaker wanting to explore her talents and abilities outside home. Or it could be an entrepreneur looking to start his business or grow his products or services into the right market. It could be someone looking to lose those few extra pounds, or it could be someone facing challenges in a relationship. It could even be an organization that wants to create the kind of employees they would fight to keep. If you are one of these people, getting a coach to support you will help you turn your vision into your reality, and help you find balance in life.

  • Is coaching same as therapy, counselling, consulting, or mentoring?

    Not at all. A consultant is a specific domain expert who can analyze your current status and provide a plan on how to do it better or right. A therapist or counselor helps you overcome psychological disorders. A mentor is someone who can directly help you get to where you want to go because she has done it herself. A coach is a qualified professional supporting normal, healthy individuals to realize their full potential and achieve sustained success and happiness. Think of a sports coach. He is not necessarily athletic or even an athlete himself. But he knows how to guide and give direction to his players to constantly improve their game. A Life Coach guides and gives direction to you to achieve success in your life.

  • I think I can benefit from coaching, but on what would I work with you?

    That is a common concern. Coaching is very dynamic in nature, and differs from individual to individual based on their needs from time to time. We usually spend a session or two to sort out priorities, and understand issues or concerns you want sorted. That too is part of coaching.

  • How long does coaching take?

    Where are you today? Where do you want to go? How fast do you want to get there? The amount of time you would need to work with your coach would depend on your answers to these three questions. Some people can achieve their goals in two to three months. Some may take a year. Coaching continues as long as the client benefits from the engagement. Generally, to experience the effectiveness of a life coaching program, we suggest clients make an initial two-month commitment. At the end of the two months, we evaluate progress and discuss the need and options for continued coaching.

    At Courtwin, our goal is to empower you to become more independent in dealing effectively with new challenges and situations, because only by becoming self-sufficient can you move forward in your goal to achieve your full potential.

  • Is there any age requirement to be your client?

    We do not have a hard and fast age criteria for accepting clients. However, coaching requires a certain amount of maturity of thought, so our recommendation is to take coaching if you are above 15 or 16 years old. On a case by case basis, we may take a client under 15 years of age, but we request at least one parent or guardian to accompany their child.

  • How much will getting a coach cost me?

    The cost of coaching varies widely on the product or package you have opted for. To give a general idea, coaching packages start from INR 7000 and go all the way up to INR 50000.
